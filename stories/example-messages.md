1. Software engineering manager in sprint planning  

“Alright team, let’s kick off planning. The main thing I want from this session is a realistic, agreed view of what we can deliver next sprint, not an aspirational wish list. Product has called out two priorities: improving onboarding conversion and stabilising the reporting backend. For onboarding, the new signup flow and the A/B test framework are top of the list. For reporting, we need to address the timeout issues on the aggregated stats endpoint before we stack more features on it.

As we go through the backlog, I’d like us to challenge anything that feels ambiguous or risky. If a ticket is under-specified or touches unknown parts of the system, call it out and we’ll either refine it here or push it out. I’d rather we commit to fewer, well understood items than overfill the sprint and carry half of them.

When you estimate, think about testing, code review, and potential integration hiccups, not just writing the code. Once we have rough sizes, we’ll look at total capacity and draw a clear line for what is in and what is out. The goal is to get to the end of the sprint with no surprises and no rollover.”

14. UX designer presenting usability test findings  

“Thanks for joining. I’d like to share the key findings from the last round of usability testing on the new onboarding flow and suggest some design changes.

We tested with eight participants, all within our target user profile. The good news is that everyone understood the overall goal of the flow and was able to complete it within a few minutes. However, three recurring issues stood out.

First, the account creation step is causing confusion. Six out of eight users hesitated when asked to choose between ‘team’ and ‘individual’ accounts, and two chose the wrong option, then backed out when they realised. A simple description under each option and setting ‘individual’ as the default would likely reduce that friction.

Second, the permissions screen is overwhelming. Users described it as ‘a wall of text’ and tended to accept the defaults without reading. We could improve this by grouping related permissions and using clearer headings and icons.

Third, the progress indicator disappears on mobile after the first step, which makes users unsure how many steps remain. Keeping a simple three step progress bar visible throughout would help.

I’ll share a short summary with proposed design tweaks after this meeting. I’d love to hear your thoughts on feasibility and any technical constraints we should consider before updating the designs.”
